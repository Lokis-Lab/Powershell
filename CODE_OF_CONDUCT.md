
# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to a welcoming, harassment-free experience for everyone who participates in this community, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, socio-economic status, or any other characteristic.

## Expected behavior

All participants are expected to:

- **Be respectful** — Disagree constructively. Assume good faith unless there is clear evidence otherwise.
- **Be inclusive** — Use welcoming language. Make space for others to speak and contribute.
- **Be professional** — Stay on topic in issues and PRs. Critique ideas and work, not people.
- **Be careful with power** — Maintainers and frequent contributors set the tone. Use that influence to uphold these standards, not to dismiss or intimidate others.
- **Respect privacy** — Do not share private information about others without consent.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following are not tolerated:

- Harassment, stalking, or intimidation (online or offline)
- Discriminatory, derogatory, or dehumanizing remarks or imagery
- Sexual attention or advances of any kind in community spaces
- Sustained disruption of discussion, events, or someone’s work
- Publishing others’ private information (doxing)
- Incitement of violence or harm toward any person or group
- Misrepresentation of identity, affiliation, or motives in bad faith
- Any conduct that could reasonably make others feel unsafe or unwelcome

This list is not exhaustive. Maintainers may address other behavior that harms the community or interferes with collaboration.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all community spaces (issues, pull requests, discussions, chat, wikis, mailing lists, and project-related meetings or events) and when an individual is officially representing the project in public.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **If you feel safe doing so**, you may ask the person to stop or point them to this document.
2. **Contact the maintainers** — Use a private channel if one is listed in the repository (for example, email or a security contact). If none is listed, open a **private** message to a maintainer or organization contact if available; avoid posting sensitive reports in public issues.

Include, to the extent you are comfortable:

- What happened, when, and where (with links if public)
- Who was involved or witnessed it
- Whether ongoing harm or retaliation is a concern

Reports are taken seriously. Maintainers will strive to protect reporters from retaliation and to handle information sensitively, subject to legal or safety obligations.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. Depending on severity, context, and repetition, responses may include:

- A private warning and request to change behavior
- A public reminder or clarification of expectations
- Temporary or permanent removal from community spaces
- Rejection or reversal of contributions associated with abusive behavior
- Escalation to platform or employer contacts when appropriate and permitted

Maintainers will aim for proportionate, consistent enforcement. If you believe a decision was wrong or unfair, you may appeal by contacting the same reporting channel with your reasoning.

## Attribution

This Code of Conduct is written to be a strong, project-agnostic baseline. Projects may adapt wording for local norms or legal requirements; substantive weakening of protections should be discussed openly with the community.
